The questions we get asked

What is the best timer app for a professional kitchen?

The one that runs multiple dishes at once, announces out loud when food is ready, and repeats your own cook times. Most cooking timer apps are built for one person's phone. This one is built for a tablet mounted on the kitchen line, in the middle of service.

How do you time multiple dishes at once without mixing them up?

Run multiple timers side by side: every dish carries its name on screen and in the voice announcements, and every kitchen zone has its own ring. Five or twenty dishes can run in parallel: you know right away which one is ringing and where. No more mistaking the fish timer for the sides.

Does the app work on iPad, Android tablets and iPhone?

Yes, on all of them. A basic iPad or a cheap Android tablet, wall-mounted with the screen always on, is enough — and it also runs on iPhone and Android phones when you want a cooking timer in your pocket. You equip every station with the same app.

Does the timer announce out loud when a dish is ready?

Yes. The app announces the next step and then the finished dish, by name, out loud. Your hands stay on the food — the info reaches your ear without staring at a screen.

Can you save your own recipes and cook times?

That's the whole point. You enter your dishes, times and steps once. The app repeats your house method identically on every tablet. It never invents a cook time.

Does the app work offline, without an internet connection?

Yes. Every tablet is self-sufficient and stores everything locally. If the wifi drops mid-rush, service goes on uninterrupted.

Can one timer chain multiple cooking steps?

Yes — it's the heart of the app. A dish can have multiple named steps (sear, flip, rest) that chain automatically, with a voice announcement at every step change.

Is the app available on the App Store and Google Play, and is it free?

It's coming soon to the App Store and Google Play. It's free, with no account to create and no subscription.
